지금 맡겨진 자리에서의 순종, 그리고 부르심의 여정

오늘 맡겨진 자리에서 시작되는 선교

Mission2035는 2035년에 갑자기 시작되는 계획이 아닙니다. 하나님께서 지금 맡기신 교회와 공동체, 그리고 한 사람을 충성스럽게 섬기는 오늘의 순종에서 이미 시작되고 있습니다. 그 부르심의 전체 이야기는 mission 페이지에 있습니다.

캠퍼스에서 함께 기도하며 — 부르심은 지금 여기에서 시작됩니다.

JDM 수원지구

2020–2026 · 캠퍼스 선교와 제자 양육 · 2021–2026 · 리더 · 2025 · 학생대표

아주대학교 입학과 함께 JDM(예수제자운동) 수원지구에서 캠퍼스 선교와 공동체 생활을 시작했습니다. 캠퍼스 전도, 일대일 관계, 소그룹 말씀 모임과 리더 훈련을 통해 복음은 단순히 전달하는 정보가 아니라 한 사람과 삶을 나누며 그리스도의 제자로 세워 가는 것임을 배웠습니다.

JDM에서 받은 가장 중요한 선교적 유산은 마태복음 28장 19절의 말씀입니다. 한 사람이 한 사람을 제자로 세우고, 그 제자가 다시 다른 제자를 세우는 재생산의 삶은 Mission2035의 중심적인 방향이 되었습니다.

캠퍼스는 선교지입니다 — JDM 수원지구 캠퍼스 사역.

경원교회 청년부

2025–현재 · 청년부 대표단 · 2025–현재 · 대학부 목장장

현재 경원교회 청년부 대표단과 대학부 목장장으로 섬기고 있습니다. 목장 공동체 안에서 말씀을 나누고, 구성원들의 삶과 신앙을 돌보며, 예배와 공동체 사역을 함께 세워 가고 있습니다.

나는 장차 먼 나라에서 감당할 사명보다 지금 곁에 맡겨진 한 사람을 충성스럽게 사랑하는 것이 먼저라고 믿습니다. 현재의 교회 공동체는 선교를 기다리는 장소가 아니라, 이미 선교적 삶을 배우고 실천하는 자리입니다.

서로를 위해 기도하며 (마 28:19).

내가 배우고 있는 사역

말씀으로 사람을 세우는 사역

사람을 실제로 변화시키는 것은 리더의 능력이나 프로그램, 설득과 열심이 아니라 하나님의 말씀과 성령의 역사입니다.

그러므로 사역자는 자신의 생각을 전달하는 사람이 아니라 살아 있는 말씀을 충성스럽게 나누는 사람이어야 합니다. 내가 먼저 말씀 앞에 서고, 그 말씀에 순종하며, 맡겨진 사람도 예수 그리스도의 말씀을 듣고 따르는 제자로 살아가도록 돕는 것이 사역자의 책임이라고 믿습니다.

나는 사람을 나의 뜻에 맞는 모습으로 만들고 싶지 않습니다. 말씀 안에서 그가 하나님을 인격적으로 만나고, 자신의 부르심을 발견하며, 성령의 인도하심에 순종하도록 오래 동행하고자 합니다.

캠퍼스 소그룹 — 말씀을 함께 나누며.

한 영혼을 오래 사랑하는 사역

사역은 많은 사람을 모으거나 모임을 원활하게 운영하는 것만을 의미하지 않습니다. 한 사람의 삶과 고민을 알고, 그의 아픔과 영적 갈급함을 품으며, 하나님께서 그 사람 가운데 일하실 것을 믿고 기다리는 일입니다.

한 영혼이 변화되고 제자로 세워지는 일에는 오랜 기도와 사랑, 인내와 희생이 필요합니다. 나 역시 많은 사람의 기도와 헌신을 통해 지금까지 세워졌기에, 이제는 하나님께서 내게 맡기신 사람들을 위해 나의 시간과 마음을 기꺼이 내어놓고자 합니다.

그러나 사람을 변화시키는 분은 내가 아닙니다. 나는 맡겨진 사람을 사랑하고 말씀으로 동행할 뿐이며, 생명을 자라게 하고 열매를 맺게 하시는 분은 하나님이심을 믿습니다.

공동체로 감당하는 사역

나는 혼자 사명을 완수하는 선교적 영웅이 되고 싶지 않습니다. 나의 신앙과 부르심은 JDM과 교회 공동체, 간사와 목회자, 동역자와 지체들의 사랑과 기도를 통해 형성되었습니다.

그리스도의 사역은 한 사람의 이름이나 능력으로 이루어지지 않습니다. 서로 다른 은사를 가진 지체들이 한 몸을 이루어 함께 기도하고, 섬기고, 기다리며 감당하는 것입니다.

Mission2035 역시 개인적인 프로젝트가 아닙니다. 지역교회와 선교 공동체 안에서 부르심을 계속 분별하고, 기도와 파송을 받으며, 장차 현지 교회와 현지의 제자들을 존중하는 동역자로 살아가고자 합니다.

부흥하는 캠퍼스, 열매맺는 제자 — JDM 수원지구 공동체.

낮아져 함께 살아가는 선교

선교는 더 높은 자리로 올라가 영향력을 행사하는 일이 아니라, 그리스도께서 인간의 삶 속으로 들어오신 것처럼 다른 사람의 삶과 문화 안으로 낮아져 들어가는 일입니다.

나의 익숙한 기준과 편안함을 고집하지 않고, 현지 사람들의 언어와 문화, 생활과 아픔을 존중하며 배우고자 합니다. 먹고 입고 말하고 물질을 사용하는 작은 방식까지도 다른 사람에게 어떤 의미로 다가갈지를 섬세하게 살피는 선교사가 되고 싶습니다.

나는 그들을 나의 방식으로 변화시키러 가는 사람이 아니라, 하나님께서 이미 그 땅에서 행하고 계신 일을 발견하고 그들의 곁에서 함께 배우며 섬기는 동역자로 나아가고자 합니다.

일상에서 시작되는 선교

선교는 해외에 도착하는 순간부터 시작되지 않습니다. 지금 하나님께서 맡기신 교회와 목장, 캠퍼스와 연구실, 강의와 관계 속에서 이미 시작되고 있습니다.

현재 맡겨진 사람을 사랑하지 못하면서 미래의 선교지에 있는 사람을 사랑할 수 없고, 오늘의 학업과 책임에 충성하지 않으면서 장차 교육자와 선교사의 직분에 충성할 수 없다고 믿습니다.

따라서 연구와 교육은 미래 선교를 위한 준비인 동시에 하나님께 드리는 현재의 예배입니다. 정직하게 연구하고, 맡겨진 책임에 최선을 다하며, 함께하는 사람들을 존중하고 섬기는 삶을 통해 지금 있는 자리에서부터 그리스도의 제자로 살아가고자 합니다.

거리에서 복음을 전하며 — 함께 기도로 준비하는 시간.

열매를 하나님께 맡기는 사역

사역의 성공은 내가 계획한 결과가 나타나는 것으로 결정되지 않습니다. 복음을 전해도 즉시 반응이 없을 수 있고, 오랫동안 사랑한 사람에게서 눈에 보이는 변화가 나타나지 않을 수도 있습니다.

그러나 구원과 변화는 전적으로 하나님의 주권과 은혜에 속해 있습니다. 내가 할 일은 결과를 만들어 내거나 소유하는 것이 아니라, 하나님의 선하심을 신뢰하며 맡겨진 자리에서 끝까지 충성하는 것입니다.

나는 씨를 뿌리고 물을 주되, 자라게 하시는 분은 하나님이심을 믿습니다. 보이는 열매가 없을 때에도 낙심하지 않고, 기도하며 말씀에 순종하고, 하나님께서 정하신 때를 기다리는 사역자가 되고자 합니다.

아직 훈련받고 있는 제자

나는 이미 준비된 선교사이거나 완성된 리더가 아닙니다. 사역과 선교의 현장에서 나의 교만과 자기중심성, 섬세하지 못함과 사랑의 부족을 계속 발견해 왔습니다.

그러나 하나님께서는 실패와 고난, 공동체 안의 부딪힘과 일상의 책임을 통해 나를 깎고 다듬어 가십니다. 나는 나의 부족함을 감추기보다 인정하고, 말씀과 기도와 공동체의 권면을 통해 계속 배우고 변화되고자 합니다.

Mission2035를 준비하는 가장 중요한 과정은 학위와 경력을 얻는 것만이 아닙니다. 한 사람을 사랑할 수 있는 인격, 다른 문화를 존중할 수 있는 겸손, 보이지 않는 자리에서 섬길 수 있는 충성, 결과를 하나님께 맡길 수 있는 믿음을 갖춘 제자로 빚어지는 것입니다.

  • 나의 이름이나 성취가 아니라 오직 예수 그리스도만 드러나도록

Mission That Begins in the Place Entrusted Today

Mission2035 is not a plan that suddenly begins in 2035. It has already begun in today’s obedience — faithfully serving the church and community, and the one person, God has entrusted to me now. The whole story of that calling is on the mission page.

Praying together on campus — the calling begins here and now.

JDM Suwon District

2020–2026 · Campus mission and discipleship · 2021–2026 · Leader · 2025 · Student representative

When I entered Ajou University, I began campus mission and community life in the Suwon district of JDM (Jesus Disciple Movement). Through campus evangelism, one-to-one relationships, small-group Bible study, and leader training, I learned that the gospel is not merely information to be delivered, but a matter of sharing life with one person and building them up as a disciple of Christ.

The most important missional legacy I received in JDM is the word of Matthew 28:19. The reproductive life — one person raising another as a disciple, and that disciple in turn raising yet another — became the central direction of Mission2035.

The campus is a mission field — JDM Suwon district campus ministry.

Kyungwon Church Young Adults

2025–present · Young-adult leadership team · 2025–present · College small-group leader

I currently serve on the young-adult leadership team and as a college small-group (“mokjang”) leader at Kyungwon Church. Within the small-group community I share the word, care for the members’ lives and faith, and help build up worship and community ministry together.

I believe that loving faithfully the one person entrusted to me now comes before the mission I will one day carry in a distant land. My present church community is not a place waiting for mission, but a place where I already learn and practice a missional life.

Praying for one another (Matt. 28:19).

The Ministry I Am Learning

Ministry That Builds People Through the Word

What actually changes a person is not a leader’s ability, a program, persuasion, or zeal, but the word of God and the work of the Holy Spirit.

So a minister must not be someone who conveys their own ideas, but one who faithfully shares the living word. I believe a minister’s responsibility is to stand first before the word, to obey it, and to help the person entrusted to me also live as a disciple who hears and follows the word of Jesus Christ.

I do not want to shape people into a form that suits my own will. Within the word, I want to walk with them for a long time so that they meet God personally, discover their own calling, and obey the leading of the Holy Spirit.

A campus small group — sharing the word together.

Ministry That Loves One Soul for a Long Time

Ministry does not mean only gathering many people or running meetings smoothly. It is knowing one person’s life and struggles, embracing their pain and spiritual thirst, and believing and waiting for God to work within them.

For one soul to be changed and built up as a disciple requires long prayer and love, patience and sacrifice. Because I too have been built up until now through the prayers and devotion of many, I now want to gladly lay down my time and heart for the people God has entrusted to me.

Yet the one who changes people is not me. I only love the person entrusted to me and walk with them through the word; the one who makes life grow and bear fruit is God.

Ministry Carried by Community

I do not want to become a missional hero who completes the mission alone. My faith and calling were formed through the love and prayers of JDM and the church community, staff and pastors, co-workers and fellow members.

The ministry of Christ is not accomplished by one person’s name or ability. It is members with different gifts forming one body — praying, serving, and waiting together.

Mission2035, too, is not a personal project. Within the local church and mission community, I want to keep discerning the calling, to receive prayer and sending, and one day to live as a co-worker who honors the local church and its disciples.

“A reviving campus, fruit-bearing disciples” — the JDM Suwon community.

Mission That Lowers Itself and Lives Alongside

Mission is not rising to a higher position to exert influence, but — just as Christ entered human life — lowering oneself to enter another person’s life and culture.

Rather than insisting on my own familiar standards and comfort, I want to respect and learn the language and culture, the life and pain of local people. I want to be a missionary who attends carefully even to the small ways of eating, dressing, speaking, and using possessions, considering what they may mean to others.

I go not as one who changes them by my own methods, but as a co-worker who discovers what God is already doing in that land and learns and serves alongside them.

Mission That Begins in Daily Life

Mission does not begin the moment one arrives overseas. It has already begun within the church and small group, the campus and lab, the lectures and relationships God has entrusted to me now.

I believe that I cannot love a person on a future mission field if I fail to love the person entrusted to me now, and that I cannot be faithful in the office of educator and missionary if I am not faithful in today’s studies and responsibilities.

So research and teaching are at once preparation for future mission and present worship offered to God. Through honest research, giving my best to the responsibilities entrusted to me, and respecting and serving those alongside me, I want to live as a disciple of Christ from the very place I am now.

Sharing the gospel on the street — preparing together in prayer.

Ministry That Entrusts the Fruit to God

The success of ministry is not determined by my planned results appearing. There may be no immediate response even when the gospel is shared, and no visible change even in a person I have long loved.

But salvation and change belong entirely to God’s sovereignty and grace. My task is not to produce or possess results, but to trust God’s goodness and remain faithful to the end in the place entrusted to me.

I believe I sow the seed and water it, but the one who makes it grow is God. Even when there is no visible fruit, I want to be a minister who does not lose heart, who prays and obeys the word, and who waits for the time God has appointed.

A Disciple Still in Training

I am not an already-prepared missionary or a finished leader. On the field of ministry and mission I have continually discovered my own pride and self-centeredness, my lack of sensitivity and love.

But God carves and shapes me through failure and hardship, through friction within community and the responsibilities of daily life. Rather than hiding my inadequacies, I want to acknowledge them and keep learning and changing through the word, prayer, and the counsel of community.

The most important part of preparing for Mission2035 is not only earning a degree and career. It is being shaped into a disciple with the character to love one person, the humility to respect another culture, the faithfulness to serve in unseen places, and the faith to entrust the results to God.

Prayer Requests

  • That I would faithfully serve to the end the community and the one person God has entrusted to me
  • That academic preparation and spiritual maturity would not be separated
  • That the way toward a doctorate abroad and long-term mission would open in God’s timing
  • That God would clearly lead me to the country and university He will send me to, and to the local church I will partner with
  • That not my name or achievement, but only Jesus Christ, would be revealed

A Journey of Calling and Obedience

부르심을 따라 제자로, 보내심을 따라 열방으로.

이 페이지는 제가 이루어 온 성취를 나열하기 위한 연혁이 아닙니다. 하나님께서 말씀과 공동체, 사역과 학업, 만남과 실패를 통해 어떻게 저를 예수 그리스도의 제자로 부르시고 Mission2035의 길로 인도하셨는지를 기억하기 위한 기록입니다.

Mission2035는 어느 날 갑자기 시작된 계획이 아닙니다. 하나님께서는 오래전부터 JDM 공동체와 교회, 캠퍼스 사역과 선교 경험, 학업과 연구를 통해 저를 준비시키셨습니다. 그리고 2026년 7월 18일, 흩어져 있던 지난 시간들이 하나의 부르심으로 연결되었습니다.

A Journey of Calling and Obedience

Called to follow as a disciple; sent to the nations.

This page is not a résumé listing achievements I have accumulated. It is a record to remember how God — through the word and community, ministry and study, encounters and failures — called me as a disciple of Jesus Christ and led me onto the road of Mission2035.

Mission2035 is not a plan that began one day. Long beforehand, God prepared me through the JDM community and the church, campus ministry and mission experience, study and research. And on July 18, 2026, the scattered seasons of the past were connected into a single calling.

CALLING DAY
2026. 07. 18
The day Mission2035 became a clear calling.

The day when God — who had been preparing me through the word and community, ministry and research — clearly confirmed the call to live, beginning in 2035, as an educator and self-supporting campus missionary at a university among the nations. After this day, Mission2035 was no longer a vague hope, but a concrete calling to prepare for and obey from today.

The Road to Mission2035

2020.03
The disciple's road that began on campus

When I entered Ajou University, I met the JDM Suwon district community. At first I thought the important thing was to keep my faith through university life and to find a good community.

But as I experienced the word and community life in JDM, I learned that a Christian is not meant to remain someone who merely guards their own faith, but is called to share the gospel and raise another as a disciple. I began to learn that the campus is not a place for preparing studies and a career, but a mission field where the gospel must be shared and disciples raised.

2021 – 2026
Serving as a leader, learning to love one person

From 2021 I served as a leader in the JDM Suwon district. Through leadership I learned that ministry is not leading people or running programs. It was knowing one person's life and struggles, praying for them, sharing word and life, and waiting a long time in faith that God would work within them.

I experienced again and again that what changes a person is not a leader's ability but the word of God and the work of the Holy Spirit. At the same time I realized that I too am not a finished leader, but a disciple who must keep being trained and changed through the people entrusted to me.

2022.03 – 2023.12
Learning faithfulness in the ordinary place

I served my military duty in the Republic of Korea Air Force. The military was not a conspicuous, special place like mission or ministry, but God taught me that even within the responsibilities and relationships of daily life I must live as a disciple of Christ.

I learned that the calling is not obedience only in special moments of ministry, but a life of faithfully carrying out entrusted responsibility in a place no one notices, and of respecting and serving the person beside me.

2024
Asking about the calling before the word

Through the Luke Bible conference and various meditations on the word, I deeply faced the question, "Why must I live as a disciple?" The answer was not my zeal or will.

It was because God first loved and saved me, and called me as a disciple of Jesus Christ. I realized that discipleship is not a condition for earning salvation, but a life-response offered to the Lord who called me by one who has become a child of God through grace. From this point, the calling became not the language of career choice, but a matter of identity and obedience.

2024
Learning the weight of one soul

Through summer and winter conferences and small-group ministry, I learned how difficult and precious it is to serve one soul. A person's life is not easily changed by a short exhortation or a single conference. For someone to offer themselves wholly before God required long prayer and waiting, love and sacrifice.

I realized that I too had been built up through the prayers and devotion of many staff, leaders, and community members. I learned that mission is not gathering many people, but loving to the end the one person God has entrusted.

2024
A first look toward the nations

Through a vision trip and mission experience, I learned that mission does not end with a special trip or emotional devotion. The challenge received on the field had to continue — back in the everyday campus and community — as obedience in serving one person.

I also began to learn that mission is not delivering what I have one-sidedly, but — just as Christ entered human life — lowering oneself to enter another person's culture and life.

2024.05 – 2024.12
Discovering service through medicine and research

Serving as a research intern in the Department of Psychiatry at Ajou University Hospital, I experienced the field where medicine and artificial intelligence connect with real human lives. I came to see that research is not an activity for developing technology or producing papers, but a domain that can serve patients and clinicians more honestly and responsibly.

This experience later led me toward researching trustworthy and interpretable medical AI.

2025
A disciple who takes responsibility for community

In 2025 I served as the student representative of the JDM Suwon district. I learned that building up a community cannot be carried by one person's passion or ability alone. Members with different gifts and dispositions had to pray, serve, and wait together to form the body of Christ.

I learned that I am not someone who completes ministry alone, but someone who takes part in God's work together with the community. Mission2035, too, I confess, is not a personal project but a calling to be discerned within the church and mission community, and carried out having received prayer and sending.

2025 – present
Today's obedience continuing at Kyungwon Church

I began serving on the young-adult leadership team and as a college small-group leader at Kyungwon Church. The disciple-making life learned in JDM had to continue within the church community even after my JDM activity ended. I share word and life with the small-group members, care for their faith and daily life, and help build up worship and community together.

I believe that, before speaking of future mission, faithfully serving the church and the one person God has entrusted to me today comes first. Mission2035 has already begun through the small obediences carried out now in the church and community.

2025
Seeing that teaching and research can be worship

Through meeting an educator of faith, I came to see that being an educator is not merely a position for delivering knowledge and producing research results. I saw an educator who regards lecturing as worship offered to God, cares for students' lives in the lab, and witnesses to the gospel through scholarship and character.

This meeting showed me that the vision of an educator and self-supporting campus missionary is not an abstract ideal, but a calling that can actually be lived out within a real university.

2026.01
Vision trip to Country I — mission that lowers itself

I went on an overseas vision trip with the JDM Suwon district community. Through this time I learned that mission is not judging another culture by my own standards or sharing what I have one-sidedly. I had to respect the local people's language and culture, life and circumstances, and lower myself to draw near to their lives.

I came to consider even how the small ways of eating, dressing, speaking, and using possessions might come across to others. I learned that I must be not one who goes to change the people of the mission field by my own methods, but a co-worker who discovers what God is already doing in that land and learns and serves alongside them.

2026.02
Beginning to raise the tent of research

I entered the master's program in the Department of AI Convergence Network at Ajou University. Studying trustworthy and interpretable medical AI, I focus on the problem of verifying whether a high-performing model actually relies on clinically valid evidence.

Research is not merely a means of employment for future mission, but a scholarly vocation God has entrusted to me now and a present ministry that serves people and the clinical field. At the same time, research and teaching will be the tent God has given me to remain among students at universities in the nations for the long haul and to witness to the gospel.

2026.07.18 · CALLING DAY
The calling became a single direction

The discipleship learned through the word, the legacy of campus mission received in JDM, service within the church community, a heart for the nations, and the vocation of research and teaching were connected into a single direction on this day.

God clearly confirmed that He is calling me to live, beginning in 2035, as an educator and self-supporting campus missionary at a university in the country to which He sends me. After this day, Mission2035 was no longer a vague hope, but a concrete calling to prepare for and obey from today.

2026 – 2028
Preparing spirituality and character, scholarship and expertise

During the master's program, I want to build expertise in trustworthy medical AI research while, within the church and community, serving the people entrusted to me and preparing the character and spirituality of a missionary.

What Mission2035 requires is not only a degree and research output. The character to love one person, the humility to respect another culture, the faith to entrust results to God, the maturity to work with community, and the attitude of remaining faithful to the end in entrusted study and research must all be prepared together.

2028 – 2035
Preparing toward universities in the nations

Through a doctorate abroad I want to build the expertise of research and teaching even more deeply. The doctorate is not a time for gaining a higher degree and a stable career, but a process of acquiring the competence and character to actually serve a nation's university and its students one day.

During this period I want to discern the country and university God will send me to, learn the necessary language and culture, and have my calling confirmed and my sending prepared within the local church and mission community.

2035
🌍 To the university God sends

Beginning in 2035, I want to live as an educator and self-supporting campus missionary at a university in the country to which God sends me. I want to teach and research, help students learn and grow, and partner with the local church to share the gospel and raise disciples. The vision of Mission2035 is to help the disciples who are built up in turn serve their own friends and juniors, campus and people, and the next generation.

I am not the savior of that land but a witness of the gospel; not the owner of God's kingdom but a servant of it. I will not try to possess results, but remain faithful to the end in the place God entrusts, leaving all the fruit to Him.
